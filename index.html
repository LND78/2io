Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Multiple Choice Questions

Q1. For any two positive integers \(a\) and \(b\), which of the following is true?

(A) \(HCF(a,b) \times LCM(a,b) = a \times b\)
(B) \(HCF(a,b) = a \times b\)
(C) \(LCM(a,b) = a \times b\)
(D) \(HCF(a,b) \times LCM(a,b) = a + b\)
Answer: (A)

Q2. The decimal expansion of the rational number \(\frac{43}{2^2 \times 5^3}\) will terminate after how many decimal places?

(A) 1
(B) 2
(C) 3
(D) 4
Answer: 3 (Because denominator is \(2^2 \times 5^3\), highest power is 3)

Q3. The sum of the exponents of the prime factors in the prime factorization of 196 is:

(A) 1
(B) 2
(C) 4
(D) 6
Answer: 4 (196 = \(2^2 \times 7^2\), sum = 2+2=4)

Q4. If the decimal expansion of a rational number is non-terminating repeating, then the number is:

(A) Integer
(B) Natural Number
(C) Irrational
(D) Rational
Answer: (D) Rational

Q5. The HCF of 96 and 404 is:

(A) 16
(B) 12
(C) 8
(D) 4
Answer: 4

Q6. According to Euclid's division lemma, for any two positive integers \(a\) and \(b\), there exist unique integers \(q\) and \(r\) such that \(a = bq + r\), where \(r\) satisfies:

(A) \(1 < r < b\)
(B) \(0 < r \le b\)
(C) \(0 \le r < b\)
(D) \(0 < r < b\)
Answer: (C) \(0 \le r < b\)

Q7. Express 156 as a product of its prime factors:

Answer: \(2^2 \times 3 \times 13\)

Q8. The decimal expansion of the number \(\frac{17}{8}\) is:

(A) Terminating
(B) Non-terminating
(C) Non-terminating recurring
(D) None of these
Answer: (A) Terminating (Since 8 = \(2^3\))

Short Answer Type Questions

Q9. For two numbers \(a\) and \(b\), write the relationship between their HCF and LCM.

Answer: \(HCF(a,b) \times LCM(a,b) = a \times b\)

Q10. Write the prime factorization of 5005.

Answer: \(5 \times 7 \times 11 \times 13\)

Q11. If the HCF of 96 and 404 is 4, find their LCM.

Answer: 9696 (\(LCM = \frac{96 \times 404}{4}\))

Q12. Check whether \(6^n\) can end with the digit 0 for any natural number \(n\).

Answer: No (Because prime factors of 6 are 2 and 3, it lacks 5)

Q13. Define HCF (Highest Common Factor).

Answer: Product of the smallest power of each common prime factor in the numbers.

Q14. Explain why \(7 \times 11 \times 13 + 13\) is a composite number.

Answer: It can be written as \(13(7 \times 11 + 1) = 13 \times 78\), so it has factors other than 1 and itself.

Q15. Find the HCF and LCM of 12, 15, and 21 using the prime factorization method.

Answer: HCF = 3, LCM = 420

Q16. Prove that \(3\sqrt{2}\) is an irrational number.

(Proof required. Assume rational, find contradiction.)

Q17. Use Euclid's division algorithm to find the HCF of 135 and 225.

Answer: 45

Q18. Use Euclid's division algorithm to find the HCF of 196 and 38220.

Answer: 196

Long Answer Type Questions

Q19. Prove that \(\sqrt{3}\) is an irrational number.

(Standard Proof: Assume \(\sqrt{3} = a/b\), square both sides, show 3 divides \(a\) and \(b\), contradiction.)

Q20. Prove that \(5 - \sqrt{3}\) is an irrational number.

(Proof: Assume rational \(r\). \(\sqrt{3} = 5 - r\). LHS is irrational, RHS is rational. Contradiction.)

Q21. Prove that \(7\sqrt{5}\) is an irrational number.

(Proof: Assume rational \(r\). \(\sqrt{5} = r/7\). LHS is irrational, RHS is rational. Contradiction.)

Q22. Given that \(HCF(306, 657) = 9\), find \(LCM(306, 657)\).

Answer: 22338

Q23. Find the LCM and HCF of 6, 72, and 120 using the prime factorization method.

Answer: HCF = 6, LCM = 360
