Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Multiple Choice Questions

Q1. For any two positive integers \(a\) and \(b\), which of the following is true?

(A) \(HCF(a,b) \times LCM(a,b) = a \times b\)
(B) \(HCF(a,b) = a \times b\)
(C) \(LCM(a,b) = a \times b\)
(D) \(HCF(a,b) \times LCM(a,b) = a + b\)
Answer: (A)

Q2. The decimal expansion of the rational number \(\frac{43}{2^2 \times 5^3}\) will terminate after how many decimal places?

(A) 1
(B) 2
(C) 3
(D) 4
Answer: 3 (Because denominator is \(2^2 \times 5^3\), highest power is 3)

Q3. The sum of the exponents of the prime factors in the prime factorization of 196 is:

(A) 1
(B) 2
(C) 4
(D) 6
Answer: 4 (196 = \(2^2 \times 7^2\), sum = 2+2=4)

Q4. If the decimal expansion of a rational number is non-terminating repeating, then the number is:

(A) Integer
(B) Natural Number
(C) Irrational
(D) Rational
Answer: (D) Rational

Q5. The HCF of 96 and 404 is:

(A) 16
(B) 12
(C) 8
(D) 4
Answer: 4

Q6. According to Euclid's division lemma, for any two positive integers \(a\) and \(b\), there exist unique integers \(q\) and \(r\) such that \(a = bq + r\), where \(r\) satisfies:

(A) \(1 < r < b\)
(B) \(0 < r \le b\)
(C) \(0 \le r < b\)
(D) \(0 < r < b\)
Answer: (C) \(0 \le r < b\)

Q7. Express 156 as a product of its prime factors:

Answer: \(2^2 \times 3 \times 13\)

Q8. The decimal expansion of the number \(\frac{17}{8}\) is:

(A) Terminating
(B) Non-terminating
(C) Non-terminating recurring
(D) None of these
Answer: (A) Terminating (Since 8 = \(2^3\))

Short Answer Type Questions

Q9. For two numbers \(a\) and \(b\), write the relationship between their HCF and LCM.

Answer: \(HCF(a,b) \times LCM(a,b) = a \times b\)

Q10. Write the prime factorization of 5005.

Answer: \(5 \times 7 \times 11 \times 13\)

Answer: 9696 (\(LCM = \frac{96 \times 404}{4}\))

Q12. Check whether \(6^n\) can end with the digit 0 for any natural number \(n\).

Answer: No (Because prime factors of 6 are 2 and 3, it lacks 5)

Q13. Define HCF (Highest Common Factor).

Answer: Product of the smallest power of each common prime factor in the numbers.

Q14. Explain why \(7 \times 11 \times 13 + 13\) is a composite number.

Answer: It can be written as \(13(7 \times 11 + 1) = 13 \times 78\), so it has factors other than 1 and itself.

Q15. Find the HCF and LCM of 12, 15, and 21 using the prime factorization method.

Answer: HCF = 3, LCM = 420

Answer: 45

Long Answer Type Questions

Q19. Prove that \(\sqrt{3}\) is an irrational number.

(Standard Proof: Assume \(\sqrt{3} = a/b\), square both sides, show 3 divides \(a\) and \(b\), contradiction.)

Q20. Prove that \(5 - \sqrt{3}\) is an irrational number.

(Proof: Assume rational \(r\). \(\sqrt{3} = 5 - r\). LHS is irrational, RHS is rational. Contradiction.)

Q21. Prove that \(7\sqrt{5}\) is an irrational number.

(Proof: Assume rational \(r\). \(\sqrt{5} = r/7\). LHS is irrational, RHS is rational. Contradiction.)

Answer: HCF = 6, LCM = 360

Chapter 2: Polynomials

Key Points

  • Polynomial: An algebraic expression in which the power of the variable is a whole number is called a polynomial. The highest power of the variable is called the degree of the polynomial.
  • Linear Polynomial: A polynomial of degree 1. General form: \(p(x) = ax + b\) (\(a \neq 0\)).
  • Quadratic Polynomial: A polynomial of degree 2. General form: \(p(x) = ax^2 + bx + c\) (\(a \neq 0\)).
  • Zeroes of a Polynomial: A real number \(k\) is a zero of a polynomial \(p(x)\) if \(p(k) = 0\). The number of zeroes is equal to the degree of the polynomial.
  • Relationship between Zeroes and Coefficients:
    • For Linear Polynomial \(ax + b\): Zero = \(-b/a\).
    • For Quadratic Polynomial \(ax^2 + bx + c\) with zeroes \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\):
      Sum of zeroes (\(\alpha + \beta\)) = \(-b/a\)
      Product of zeroes (\(\alpha\beta\)) = \(c/a\)
  • Quadratic Formula: If sum (\(S\)) and product (\(P\)) of zeroes are given, the polynomial is \(k[x^2 - Sx + P]\).

Short Answer Type Questions

Q13. Find the zeroes of polynomial \(p(x) = x^2-2x-8\).

Answer: \(4, -2\).
Solution: \(x^2-4x+2x-8 \Rightarrow x(x-4)+2(x-4) \Rightarrow (x-4)(x+2) = 0 \Rightarrow x=4, -2\).

Q14. Find a quadratic polynomial whose sum and product of zeroes are -3 and 2 respectively.

Answer: \(x^2+3x+2\).
Solution: Formula \(x^2 - (\alpha+\beta)x + \alpha\beta \Rightarrow x^2 - (-3)x + 2\).

Q15. Find zeroes of quadratic polynomial \(p(x) = 6x^2-3-7x\) and verify the relationship between zeroes and coefficients.

Answer: \(3/2, -1/3\).
Solution: \(6x^2-7x-3 = 0 \Rightarrow 6x^2-9x+2x-3=0 \Rightarrow 3x(2x-3)+1(2x-3)=0 \Rightarrow (2x-3)(3x+1)=0\).

Q16. If sum of zeroes of quadratic expression \(Kx^2+5x+3k\) is equal to their product, find the value of k.

Answer: \(k = -5/3\).
Solution: Sum = \(-5/k\), Product = \(3k/k = 3\). Sum = Product \(\Rightarrow -5/k = 3 \Rightarrow k = -5/3\).

Q17. Find a quadratic polynomial the sum and product of whose zeroes are \(-1/4\) and \(1/4\) respectively.

Answer: \(4x^2+x+1\).
Solution: \(k[x^2 - (-1/4)x + (1/4)] \Rightarrow k[x^2 + x/4 + 1/4]\). Let \(k=4\), then \(4x^2+x+1\).

Q18. Find the value of polynomial \(p(x) = x^2-3x-4\) at \(x = -1\).

Answer: 0.
Solution: \(p(-1) = (-1)^2 - 3(-1) - 4 = 1 + 3 - 4 = 0\).

Q19. Find the zero of the linear polynomial \(p(x) = 3x-4\).

Answer: \(4/3\).
Solution: \(3x-4=0 \Rightarrow 3x=4 \Rightarrow x=4/3\).
